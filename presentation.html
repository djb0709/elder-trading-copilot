Total Return
WHATOverall % gain or loss between starting capital and final equity.
FORMULA(final_equity − capital) / capital × 100
CODE(equity_curve[-1] - capital) / capital * 100
EXAMPLE$10,000 → $16,565  =  +65.65%
SHARPE RATIO
0.60
Sharpe Ratio
WHATReturn per unit of risk, annualized. Higher = more return per unit of volatility.
FORMULAmean(daily_ret) / std(daily_ret) × √252
NOTERisk-free rate assumed = 0. 252 = trading days/year.
READ<1 weak  ·  1–2 good  ·  >2 excellent
MAX DRAWDOWN
−33%
Max Drawdown
WHATThe worst peak-to-trough equity decline — how far you'd be down at your lowest point.
FORMULAmax((peak − equity) / peak) × 100
CODEpeak = equity.cummax() · running maximum
EXAMPLE$15k → $10k  =  33% drawdown. Tells you how bad it felt to hold.
RISK LEVEL
HIGH
Risk Level
WHATCategorical severity tag derived directly from Max Drawdown.
RULES Low if MaxDD < 15%  ·  Med if < 30%  ·  High if ≥ 30%
WHYRaw drawdown % is abstract — a label communicates severity at a glance.

INDICATOR · SCREEN 01
EMA
Exponential Moving Average · trend smoother

An EMA is a smoothed average of the last N days of price, weighted so recent prices count more. It filters out daily noise and shows you the underlying direction.

"Short" and "long" refer to the window length. A 12-day EMA reacts quickly and hugs the current price. A 26-day EMA is slower and represents the bigger-picture average.

When the short EMA sits above the long EMA, recent prices are beating the longer-term average — that's an uptrend. The opposite means a downtrend.

▸ Traders call this crossing a "golden cross" (up) or "death cross" (down).

▸ SIGNAL   EMA_short > EMA_long   ⟹   major trend is up · Screen 1 passes
INDICATOR · SCREEN 02
MACD
Moving Average Convergence Divergence · momentum gauge

MACD = EMA_12 − EMA_26. It's literally the difference between a fast EMA and a slow EMA, turned into its own line.

A second line, the signal line, is a 9-period EMA of the MACD line itself. Think of it as "MACD's own trend".

When the MACD line is above its signal line, momentum is building — the short EMA is pulling away from the long EMA faster than before. The histogram bars visualize the gap.

▸ EMA tells you the direction. MACD tells you whether that direction is gaining or losing steam.

▸ SIGNAL   MACD > MACD_signal   ⟹   momentum confirms · Screen 2 passes
INDICATOR · SCREEN 03
N-day Breakout
Price-based entry trigger

A breakout is the simplest possible entry rule: we find the highest closing price over the last N days, and we only buy if today's close rises above it.

Why wait for this? Because trend + momentum can both look right, but the market can still stall. A breakout forces price itself to prove the setup before our money is on the line.

In the slider, Breakout Window is that N. Smaller N → more aggressive, more trades. Larger N → stricter, fewer but cleaner entries.

▸ Elder's original Screen 3 used an intraday trailing buy-stop. We use a daily close-based breakout — simpler and works on end-of-day data.

▸ SIGNAL   close > max(close[−N:])   ⟹   enter long position
INDICATOR · EXIT
RSI
Relative Strength Index · overbought / oversold meter

RSI measures how strong recent gains are compared to recent losses, and maps it onto a 0 to 100 scale.

Above 70 means the market has been rallying hard — "overbought", likely to reverse down. Below 30 means it's been selling off hard — "oversold", likely to bounce back up.

We don't use RSI to enter (that's what EMA, MACD, and Breakout are for). We use it to exit — when RSI hits an extreme, we close the position before the reversal eats our profit.

▸ The dashboard sliders RSI Lower and RSI Upper let you set your own overbought/oversold thresholds.

▸ SIGNAL   RSI > upper  ||  RSI < lower   ⟹   exit position
